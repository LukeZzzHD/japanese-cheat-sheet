## Particles (じょし)

| Particle | Romaji | Usage | Example |
|----------|--------|-------|---------|
| は | wa | Topic marker | わたし**は** がくせい です (watashi **wa** gakusei desu) - I am a student |
| を | o | Object marker | ごはん**を** たべます (gohan **o** tabemasu) - I eat rice |
| で | de | Location of action | レストラン**で** たべます (resutoran **de** tabemasu) - I eat at a restaurant |
| に | ni | Direction/time/location | いえ**に** かえります (ie **ni** kaerimasu) - I return home |
| も | mo | Also/too | ビール**も** ください (biiru **mo** kudasai) - Beer too, please |
| が | ga | Subject marker | にほんご**が** できます (nihongo **ga** dekimasu) - I can speak Japanese |
| の | no | Possessive | わたし**の** かぞく (watashi **no** kazoku) - My family |
| と | to | With (someone) / And | ちち**と** はは**と** わたし (chichi **to** haha **to** watashi) - Father, mother, and me |
| から | kara | From | どちら**から**？ (dochira **kara**?) - Where are you from? |

## Grammar Patterns (ぶんぽう)

### Basic Sentence Structure
**[Topic] は [Object] を [Verb]**

Example:
- わたし は ごはん を たべます
- watashi wa gohan o tabemasu
- I eat rice

### Polite Verb Conjugation (-masu form)
| Form | Pattern | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| Positive | ~ます (~masu) | たべます (tabemasu) - eat |
| Negative | ~ません (~masen) | たべません (tabemasen) - don't eat |
| Past Positive | ~ました (~mashita) | かいました (kaimashita) - bought |
| Past Negative | ~ませんでした (~masendeshita) | たべませんでした - didn't eat |

### Self-Introduction Pattern (じこしょうかい)
- はじめまして (hajimemashite) - Nice to meet you (first meeting)
- わたし は [Name] です (watashi wa [Name] desu) - I am [Name]
- [Country] から きました ([Country] kara kimashita) - I came from [Country]
- どうぞ よろしく (douzo yoroshiku) - Nice to meet you

### Asking Someone's Name
- おなまえは？ (onamae wa?) - What is your name?
- [Name] です ([Name] desu) - I am [Name]

### Where Are You From? (どちらから)
- どちらから？ (dochira kara?) - Where are you from?
- [Country] です ([Country] desu) - I'm from [Country]
- そうですか (soo desu ka) - I see / Is that so?

### Ability Pattern (できます)
- [Language]が できます ([Language] ga dekimasu) - I can speak [Language]
- はい、できます (hai, dekimasu) - Yes, I can
- はい、すこし できます (hai, sukoshi dekimasu) - Yes, I can a little
- べんきょうちゅうです (benkyou-chuu desu) - I'm currently studying it
- いいえ、できません (iie, dekimasen) - No, I can't

### Occupation Pattern (しごと)
- おしごとは？ (oshigoto wa?) - What is your job?
- [Job] です ([Job] desu) - I am a [Job]
- わたしは しゅふ です (watashi wa shufu desu) - I'm a housewife

### Family Size Pattern
- かぞくは [Number]にん です (kazoku wa [Number]-nin desu) - My family has [Number] people
- ちちと ははと わたし です (chichi to haha to watashi desu) - It's my father, mother, and me

### Living Location Pattern
- [Place]に すんでいます ([Place] ni sundeimasu) - I live in [Place]
- おおさかに すんでいます (oosaka ni sundeimasu) - I live in Osaka

### Likes/Dislikes Pattern
- [Thing] が すき です ([Thing] ga suki desu) - I like [Thing]
- [Thing] が きらい です ([Thing] ga kirai desu) - I dislike [Thing]
- すきな りょうり は なん です か (sukina ryouri wa nan desu ka) - What is your favorite dish?

### Location of Action (で de)
- [Place] で [Verb]
- レストラン で たべます (resutoran de tabemasu) - I eat at the restaurant

### Time Expression Pattern
- [Time] に [Verb]
- 7じ に おきます (shichi-ji ni okimasu) - I wake up at 7 o'clock

### Activity Pattern (を します)
- [Activity] を します
- べんきょう を します (benkyou o shimasu) - I study
- うんどう を します (undou o shimasu) - I exercise

### Haven't Decided Yet
- まだ きめて いません (mada kimete imasen) - I haven't decided yet

### Preference Patterns (すきですか)
- なにが すきですか (nani ga suki desu ka) - What do you like?
- [Food]が すきです ([Food] ga suki desu) - I like [Food]
- いちばん すきです (ichiban suki desu) - I like it the most
- わたしも です (watashi mo desu) - Me too

### Asking About Hobbies (しゅみ)
- しゅみは なんですか (shumi wa nan desu ka) - What is your hobby?
- どんな スポーツが すきですか (donna supootsu ga suki desu ka) - What kind of sports do you like?
- やきゅう が すきです (yakyuu ga suki desu) - I like baseball

### Invitation Pattern (さそい)
- [Day]は いいですか ([Day] wa ii desu ka) - Is [Day] good?
- いいですよ (ii desu yo) - It's fine / Sure
- ざんねんですが (zannen desu ga) - Unfortunately...
- じゃ、そうしましょう (ja, sou shimashou) - Let's do that

### Schedule/Plans Pattern
- らいしゅうの スケジュールは どうですか (raishuu no sukejuuru wa dou desu ka) - How is next week's schedule?
- [Place]に いきます ([Place] ni ikimasu) - I'll go to [Place]
- ともだちと あいます (tomodachi to aimasu) - I'll meet with a friend

### Opinion Pattern (いけん)
- はやいですね (hayai desu ne) - That's early
- おそいですね (osoi desu ne) - That's late
- おいしいですよ (oishii desu yo) - It's delicious (assertion)
- あの みせ は おいしい です よ (ano mise wa oishii desu yo) - That shop is delicious

### Days Off Pattern (やすみ)
- やすみのひ は なにを しますか (yasumi no hi wa nani o shimasu ka) - What do you do on your days off?
- うちで なにを しますか (uchi de nani o shimasu ka) - What do you do at home?

### After ~ Pattern (~のあとで)
- ばんごはんの あとで なにを しますか (bangohan no ato de nani o shimasu ka) - What do you do after dinner?

## Notes & Exceptions

### Frequency Adverbs with Negative Verbs
- あまり and ぜんぜん **must** be used with negative verb forms
- Example: ぜんぜん たべません (zenzen tabemasen) - I don't eat at all
- **Incorrect**: ぜんぜん たべます ✗

### い-Adjective Negative Form
- Remove い (i) and add くない (kunai)
- おいしい → おいしくない (oishii → oishikunai)
- ちいさい → ちいさくない (chiisai → chiisakunai)
- **Exception**: いい → よくない (ii → yokunai)

### Time Expressions
- ごろ (goro) = approximate point in time (e.g., 3じ ごろ = around 3 o'clock)
- ぐらい (gurai) = approximate duration (e.g., 2じかん ぐらい = about 2 hours)

### Number Sound Changes
Watch out for irregular readings:
- 300 = さん**びゃく** (sanbyaku) - not sanhyaku
- 600 = ろっ**ぴゃく** (roppyaku) - not rokuhyaku
- 800 = はっ**ぴゃく** (happyaku) - not hachihyaku
- 3000 = さん**ぜん** (sanzen) - not sansen
- 8000 = はっ**せん** (hassen) - not hachisen

### Counting People
- 1 person = ひとり (hitori) - irregular
- 2 people = ふたり (futari) - irregular
- 3+ people = number + にん (nin)

### Days of the Month
Irregular readings for dates 1-10, 14, 20, 24:
- 1日 = ついたち (tsuitachi)
- 2日 = ふつか (futsuka)
- 3日 = みっか (mikka)
- etc.

Regular pattern for most other days: number + にち (nichi)

### Family Vocabulary
- Use humble forms (ちち, はは) when talking about YOUR family
- Use polite forms (おとうさん, おかあさん) when talking about SOMEONE ELSE'S family

### Win/Lose
| Japanese | Romaji | English |
|----------|--------|---------|
| かち | kachi | win |
| まけ | make | lose |
